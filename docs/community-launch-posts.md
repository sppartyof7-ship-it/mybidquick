## 3. Reddit r/SaaS

**Title:** I built a vertical SaaS quoting tool for cleaning companies — here's what I learned

**Body:**
Been building MyBidQuick — an instant online quoting tool for exterior cleaning companies (pressure washing, window cleaning, gutter cleaning, etc.).

The problem: Cleaning company owners spend 5-10 hours per week on quoting. They drive to properties, measure, build quotes in spreadsheets, email them out — and by then the customer picked someone faster. 78% of customers go with the first company that gives them a price.

The solution: An embeddable quoting tool where customers enter their address, pick services, and get an instant branded quote in 60 seconds. The cleaning company gets the lead automatically.

A few things I've learned going vertical in SaaS:

1. Industry-specific beats general-purpose every time. These business owners don't want another CRM. They want something that speaks their language — house wash, gutter cleaning, soft wash.

2. Tiered pricing is a game changer. Offering Standard/Premium/Platinum packages with cascade upsells (house wash → add gutters → add windows) increases average ticket 30-50%.

3. Mobile-first is non-negotiable. 70%+ of quotes are requested from phones.

4. White-labeling matters more than you'd think. These owners are proud of their brand. They want their logo and colors, not yours.

Would appreciate any feedback. Still in early stages.
